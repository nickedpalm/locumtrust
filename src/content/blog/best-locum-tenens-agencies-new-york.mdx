## Why New York Is a Different Animal

NYC doesn't care about your agency's reputation in [Phoenix](/locations/arizona/phoenix/). The health systems here — Northwell, NYC Health + Hospitals, Mount Sinai, NYU Langone — have their own credentialing timelines, their own preferred vendor lists, and their own tolerance for delays. An agency that can credential a physician for a rural Tennessee hospital in two weeks might take six in New York because of the additional layers.

Here's what most people miss: New York's physician licensing requirements are stricter than most states, and the state medical board isn't known for speed. Agencies that place heavily in NY have already built workflows around this. Agencies that treat NY as one state among fifty will slow you down.

Demand is also real and ongoing. Urban density means higher patient volumes, which means more burnout, more leave coverage needs, more gaps. The market doesn't soften between assignments the way it might in a smaller metro.

## What to Actually Ask Before You Sign

The agencies on this list range from 22-year-old firms with 150+ hospital system networks to lean regional players. Size isn't the whole story. Here's the filter that matters:

**For facilities:**
- How many physicians have you placed in [your health system or county] in the past 12 months?
- What's your average time from request to credentialed, ready-to-work for New York specifically?
- Do you carry malpractice tail coverage, or does the facility?
- What's your gap-fill guarantee if a placed locum can't complete the assignment?

**For clinicians:**
- Are you a NALTO member? (It's not a guarantee of quality, but non-members have fewer accountability structures)
- What NY-specific license support do you provide?
- Can you show me active NY postings in my specialty right now?

> **Reality Check:** Any agency can claim "nationwide coverage." The real question is whether they have active relationships with NY credentialing coordinators and existing contracts with NY health systems. Ask for specifics. Vague answers are a red flag.
