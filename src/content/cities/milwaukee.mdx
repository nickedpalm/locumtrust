## How to Choose a Locum Tenens Agency in Milwaukee

- **Verify NALTO membership first.** NALTO members are bound to a code of ethics covering fee transparency, candidate exclusivity, and credentialing standards. Non-members aren't. This is a 30-second filter that eliminates a lot of noise.
- **Ask specifically about Wisconsin licensing timelines.** Wisconsin's medical licensing board runs 4–8 weeks for standard applications. An agency that can't tell you exactly how they manage expedited licensing — or which candidates they already have licensed in-state — is going to blow your timeline.
- **Confirm malpractice tail coverage in writing.** Most reputable agencies provide occurrence-based coverage, but some slip in claims-made policies that leave you exposed after the engagement ends. Get the coverage type in the contract, not just the certificate.
- **Match specialty depth to your gap.** A generalist agency might have 12 hospitalists available but zero pediatric cardiologists. Froedtert's referral volume means Milwaukee often needs subspecialty coverage fast — confirm the agency has recent placements in your specific specialty, not just "we cover all specialties."
- **Ask about their guarantee policy.** Top-tier agencies will replace a provider at no additional placement fee if the fit fails within the first week. Agencies that don't offer this are pricing in their own uncertainty.

> **Pro Tip:** Milwaukee sits in a competitive Midwest corridor — [Chicago](/locations/illinois/chicago/) agencies frequently try to cover Wisconsin with providers who've never practiced here. Ask the agency when they last placed a provider specifically in Milwaukee and which facility. Vague answers are an answer.

## What to Expect

Locum contracts in Milwaukee typically run $20,000–$150,000 depending on specialty, duration, and urgency — hospitalists and primary care skew lower, while surgical subspecialties and anesthesiology push toward the top of that range. Most agencies charge a markup of 25–40% over the provider's hourly rate, covering credentialing, malpractice, housing, and travel logistics. From signed contract to first shift, expect 2–6 weeks for a licensed candidate, 6–10 weeks if licensing needs to be initiated.

> **Reality Check:** The agencies that quote you the lowest total don't always cost the least. A cheaper agency with a 10-week credentialing timeline costs your organization far more than a premium agency that gets a licensed provider on-site in three weeks. Model the cost of the coverage gap, not just the placement fee.
