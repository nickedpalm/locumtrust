Finding a reliable locum tenens agency in [Philadelphia](/locations/pennsylvania/philadelphia/) shouldn't feel like navigating the SEPTA system at rush hour — but for most CMOs and clinic administrators, it does. The Philadelphia metro runs on a dozen major health systems (Jefferson, Penn Medicine, Temple, Einstein) all competing for the same shrinking pool of temporary physicians, which means the agency you pick determines whether your ER stays functional or your patients wait three weeks for an appointment. This directory cuts through the noise.

## How to Choose a Locum Tenens Agency in Philadelphia

- **Verify NALTO membership first, ask questions second.** NALTO members are bound by a code of ethics that prohibits double-brokering and requires transparent fee disclosure. In a market this competitive, non-member agencies sometimes use opaque markup structures that inflate your total cost by 15–30%.
- **Ask specifically about Pennsylvania licensure timelines.** PA is not a Compact state for physicians, so standard licensure can run 8–14 weeks. The best agencies maintain a bench of pre-licensed Pennsylvania physicians — ask how many they have available in your specialty before you sign anything.
- **Confirm malpractice coverage structure.** Request a certificate of insurance showing occurrence-based (not claims-made) coverage. Tail coverage disputes are the most common source of post-engagement headaches; get it in writing before placement.
- **Probe their credentialing process.** Joint Commission Certified or NCQA-credentialed agencies have standardized primary source verification. For Philadelphia's major health systems, credentialing shortcuts will get your locum flagged at medical staff review — adding weeks of delay.
- **Get the guarantee policy in plain English.** If a physician leaves mid-contract, what happens? Top-tier agencies replace within 48–72 hours at no additional cost. Vague "we'll do our best" language is a red flag.

> **Pro Tip:** Philadelphia's academic medical centers (Penn, Jefferson, Temple) often require additional credentialing beyond standard hospital privileges. Ask your agency if they've placed specifically at your institution before — the paperwork differences are real and they add days.

## What to Expect

Locum tenens contracts in the Philadelphia market typically run $20,000–$150,000 depending on specialty, duration, and urgency — emergency medicine and hospitalist coverage on the high end, primary care on the lower. Most placements move from initial request to first shift in 2–6 weeks, though agencies with pre-credentialed Pennsylvania physicians can compress that significantly. Budget a 25–35% markup over the physician's daily rate as the agency's standard fee.

> **Reality Check:** The biggest pricing mistake administrators make is comparing daily rates without accounting for agency fees, travel, housing, and malpractice in a single number. Ask every agency for a fully-loaded cost estimate — all-in, per shift — so you're comparing apples to apples. Agencies that resist giving you this number are telling you something.

## Local Market Overview

Philadelphia's hospital market is dense and consolidating — Jefferson Health alone spans 18 hospitals — which creates both opportunity and pressure in locum staffing. Demand spikes predictably around the academic calendar as resident rotations shift, and the city's high patient volume in trauma and urban primary care means specialties like emergency medicine, internal medicine, and psychiatry see near-permanent locum demand. Agencies with established relationships inside the Philadelphia health systems will move faster and hit fewer credentialing snags than generalists who treat this market like any other mid-Atlantic city.
