## How to Choose a Locum Tenens Agency in Memphis

- **Verify NALTO membership first, full stop.** Non-member agencies aren't bound by the code of ethics that governs fee transparency and candidate exclusivity. Memphis has enough agency churn that this filter alone cuts your risk significantly.
- **Ask specifically about Tennessee medical licensing timelines.** Tennessee's Board of Medical Examiners runs 6–10 weeks for a standard application. Any agency promising a credentialed physician in two weeks either has someone already licensed in-state or is telling you what you want to hear.
- **Confirm they carry malpractice tail coverage.** Occurrence-based policies are preferable; claims-made policies leave gaps when a locum's engagement ends. Get this in writing before a contract is signed.
- **Demand specialty depth, not just headcount.** A generalist agency with 40,000 providers sounds better than a specialist firm with 3,000 — until you need an interventional cardiologist in Memphis in 72 hours and they're sending you family medicine candidates.
- **Check their guarantee policy.** Top-tier agencies will replace a provider at no additional cost if the placement doesn't work out within the first week. If they balk at discussing guarantees, that tells you something.

> **Pro Tip:** Memphis sits at the intersection of three major health systems competing for the same regional physician pool — UTHSC, Baptist, and Methodist all recruit from overlapping networks. An agency with established relationships in the Mid-South region will move faster than a national firm that treats Memphis as a pin on a map.

## What to Expect

Locum tenens contracts in Memphis typically run $20,000–$150,000 per engagement depending on specialty, duration, and urgency — primary care and hospitalist coverage sits toward the lower range, while surgical subspecialties and anesthesiology push the ceiling. The standard process runs 2–4 weeks from initial request to boots-on-the-ground: intake call, candidate submission, credentialing verification, and travel/housing logistics handled by the agency.

> **Reality Check:** The biggest pricing mistake administrators make is comparing daily rates without accounting for agency-covered costs — malpractice insurance, housing, travel, and licensing fees can add $300–$600/day in hidden value that looks "expensive" on the rate sheet but would cost you more to manage in-house. Compare total cost of engagement, not the bill rate in isolation.

Memphis's healthcare landscape is shaped by UTHSC's academic medical ecosystem and a regional referral draw that pulls patients from rural West Tennessee, North Mississippi, and Eastern Arkansas — meaning your coverage gaps affect more than just your patient panel. The city's historically elevated physician-to-population ratio challenges mean locum demand here is structural, not seasonal, and agencies that understand the Mid-South market will credential faster and retain better than those treating it like a short-term arbitrage opportunity.
