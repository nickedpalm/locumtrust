## How to Choose a Locum Tenens Agency in Charlotte

- **Verify NALTO membership first, not last.** NALTO members are bound to a code of ethics that covers fee transparency, candidate representation, and dispute resolution. An agency that isn't a member isn't automatically bad — but you have zero recourse when a placement falls through the week before your coverage gap starts.
- **Ask specifically about North Carolina licensing timelines.** NC Medical Board processing for locum physicians averages 60–90 days for new licenses, though endorsement can move faster. Any agency that quotes you two weeks without a solid explanation of their expedited licensing relationships is selling you something.
- **Get the credentialing SLA in writing.** Top agencies commit to primary source verification turnarounds of 5–10 business days. Vague answers here cost you when a hospital system like Atrium Health needs Joint Commission-compliant documentation before Day 1.
- **Ask about their Charlotte-specific placement history.** The Carolinas are a growth market — population influx, suburban expansion, new urgent care corridors along I-485. An agency that has placed hospitalists, ED physicians, or APPs in Charlotte's specific payer mix (heavy BCBSNC, growing Medicaid managed care) understands the local constraints better than one parachuting in.
- **Confirm malpractice structure upfront.** Occurrence-based vs. claims-made coverage is a real issue for locums. The agency should carry tail coverage as part of the standard contract — if they push it back to the provider or leave it ambiguous, that's a red flag.

> **Pro Tip:** Ask the agency for two references from Charlotte-area facilities — not just national accounts. Any agency worth hiring has placed someone within 50 miles of your facility in the last 18 months.

## What to Expect

Locum tenens contracts in Charlotte run $20,000–$150,000 depending on specialty, duration, and urgency — an EM physician covering a holiday weekend is a very different animal than a three-month hospitalist engagement while you recruit permanent staff. The typical process from signed agreement to first shift runs 3–6 weeks when licensing is already in place, longer if the provider needs a new NC license or your credentialing committee meets monthly.

> **Reality Check:** The biggest pricing mistake administrators make is comparing day rates without accounting for what's bundled. Agency A quotes $180/hour; Agency B quotes $210/hour. But Agency B includes travel, housing, malpractice, and a 30-day guarantee replacement if the placement doesn't work out. The math flips fast. Always ask for an all-in cost comparison.
