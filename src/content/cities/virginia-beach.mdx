## How to Choose a Locum Tenens Agency in Virginia Beach

- **Verify NALTO membership first.** NALTO members operate under a code of ethics that includes malpractice tail coverage and honest provider representation. Non-members aren't automatically bad, but the accountability structure is weaker — and you'll feel that absence when something goes sideways mid-contract.
- **Ask specifically about Virginia licensure timelines.** The Virginia Board of Medicine is not the fastest in the country. A credentialing team that's placed providers in Virginia before will know the current processing backlog. One that quotes you a generic "2–4 weeks" probably hasn't.
- **Check for NCQA Credentialing Accreditation or Joint Commission Certification.** These aren't marketing badges — they represent audited primary source verification processes. For a facility that accepts Medicare or is Joint Commission accredited itself, working with a certified agency is increasingly an expectation, not a differentiator.
- **Ask about their specialty bench for your specific need.** "We cover all specialties" is a sales answer. The real question is how many active locums they have credentialed and placed in that specialty in the last 12 months. Depth of bench determines how fast they can fill your gap.
- **Get the guarantee policy in writing before you sign.** Top-tier agencies offer replacement guarantees if a provider leaves mid-engagement. Know exactly what triggers it, what the response window is, and who eats the credentialing cost for the replacement.

> **Pro Tip:** Virginia Beach's proximity to Naval Station Norfolk and Langley AFB means some facilities have specific credentialing requirements for providers working near military installations. Mention this upfront — it filters out agencies that will waste your time.

## What to Expect

Locum tenens contracts in Virginia Beach typically run $20,000–$150,000 depending on specialty, duration, and urgency — emergency medicine and surgical subspecialties sit at the high end, primary care and hospitalist coverage toward the lower range. Agencies handle malpractice coverage, travel logistics, and licensing coordination, but their fee is baked into the hourly bill rate, not itemized separately, so the rate you see already includes their margin.

> **Reality Check:** The most common budgeting mistake is comparing locum bill rates to your permanent physician salary without accounting for what you're *not* paying — employer taxes, benefits, CME, PTO, and recruitment costs. A $180/hr locum hospitalist often costs less than a bad permanent hire does in the first year alone.

Expect 2–6 weeks from initial contact to first shift for most specialties, assuming Virginia licensure is already in place. Urgent fills can move faster with a telemedicine bridge or if the provider holds a compact state license.
