## How to Choose a Locum Tenens Agency in Raleigh

- **Verify NALTO membership first.** NALTO members agree to binding ethical standards around fee transparency and credentialing practices. In North Carolina, where the Medical Board has specific expedited licensure provisions for locums, a NALTO-affiliated agency will know how to navigate that process — non-members often don't.
- **Ask specifically about North Carolina licensure timelines.** NC has a full-license-required rule for locums (no special locum license). A seasoned agency should be able to quote you average NC Medical Board turnaround times and have a track record of accelerating them via the IMLCC compact pathway if your candidate holds a compact license.
- **Check their specialty depth, not just their specialty breadth.** A lot of agencies claim to cover "all specialties." Press them: how many hospitalists did they place in the Triangle last quarter? How many emergency physicians? Depth in your specialty matters more than a long dropdown menu on their website.
- **Get the malpractice coverage in writing before you sign.** Occurrence vs. claims-made is not a technicality — it's the difference between your facility being exposed after the engagement ends. Any agency worth working with will provide a certificate of insurance upfront.
- **Ask for a fill-rate guarantee.** Top-tier agencies stand behind their placements with replacement guarantees if a provider doesn't complete an engagement. If an agency hedges on this, that tells you something.

> **Pro Tip:** Raleigh sits in a high-competition Triangle market — UNC Health, Duke, WakeMed, and dozens of private practices all fish from the same provider pool. Agencies with a strong Southeast regional presence or a dedicated Triangle network will outperform national generalists on speed every time.

## What to Expect

Contract values for locum placements typically run $20,000–$150,000 depending on specialty, duration, and whether travel and housing are baked in — hospitalist and primary care engagements tend to land in the lower half of that range, while surgical subspecialties push toward the top. Credentialing typically takes 2–6 weeks for a provider already licensed in NC; first-time NC licensure adds 4–10 weeks. Most agencies can have a candidate proposal in front of you within 48–72 hours of an intake call.

> **Reality Check:** The most common pricing mistake is comparing agency bill rates without accounting for what's included. One agency's $180/hour might include malpractice, housing, and flights. Another's $160/hour might not. Model total engagement cost, not hourly rate.

## Local Market Overview

Raleigh's rapid population growth — it's one of the fastest-growing metros in the Southeast — has strained physician supply across primary care and hospital medicine, making locum placements less of a stopgap and more of a routine staffing tool for Triangle health systems. The Research Triangle's concentration of academic medical centers also means a higher-than-average share of locum providers here hold subspecialty credentials, which benefits facilities that need coverage beyond generalist medicine.
