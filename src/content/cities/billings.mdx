## How to Choose a Locum Tenens Agency in Billings

- **Verify NALTO membership first.** NALTO members are bound to a code of ethics that covers transparent fee disclosure and credentialing standards. Non-member agencies aren't necessarily bad, but you have less recourse when things go sideways.
- **Ask about Montana licensing turnaround specifically.** Montana is not a Compact state for physicians (it joined the Interstate Medical Licensure Compact, but processing times vary). An agency that places heavily in the Mountain West will have established relationships with the Montana Board of Medical Examiners and realistic timelines — don't let a sales rep low-ball this figure.
- **Demand malpractice documentation before the first shift.** The agency should carry occurrence-based or tail-covered claims-made coverage. Get the certificate, not just a verbal confirmation.
- **Check specialty depth, not just headcount.** Billings Clinic and St. Vincent Healthcare draw regional referrals from a 500-mile radius across Montana, Wyoming, and the Dakotas. You need agencies with real benches in hospitalist medicine, orthopedics, and emergency medicine — not generalist shops that happen to have one EM name.
- **Ask for references from comparable markets.** Rural-adjacent tertiary care is its own staffing challenge. An agency that's great in [Phoenix](/locations/arizona/phoenix/) may have no idea how to handle a locum who's asked to cover call in Hardin or Miles City.

> **Pro Tip:** Request the agency's average credentialing-to-first-shift timeline for Mountain West placements specifically. National averages mean nothing if their Montana pipeline is thin. Best-in-class agencies hit 30 days or under on credentialing; anyone quoting 8–10 weeks for a routine placement is burning your buffer time.

## What to Expect

Locum contracts in Billings typically run $20,000–$150,000 per engagement depending on specialty, duration, and whether the role requires call coverage — hospitalists and ER physicians skew toward the higher end given 24/7 demand. Agencies bundle their fee into the bill rate rather than charging you separately, which sounds clean until you realize you can't easily compare margins across bids. Expect a 2–6 week lead time from signed agreement to boots-on-ground for most specialties, longer if a Montana license needs to be issued from scratch.

> **Reality Check:** The "all-inclusive" rate quote you get on day one rarely survives contact with reality. Confirm upfront whether travel, housing, malpractice, and licensing fees are baked in or itemized separately — the delta can swing a contract by $15,000 or more.
