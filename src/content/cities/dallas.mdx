## How to Choose a Locum Tenens Agency in Dallas

- **Verify NALTO membership and credentialing accreditation first.** NALTO members are bound by a code of ethics that prohibits double-placing providers and requires transparent fee disclosure. NCQA Credentialing Accreditation means primary source verification isn't being done by a junior recruiter with a checklist — it's audited. Ask for the certificate, not just the claim.
- **Check specialty depth, not just headcount.** A generalist staffing firm with 10,000 providers on paper may have three hospitalists licensed in Texas. Ask specifically: how many active physicians do you have credentialed in Texas right now, in this specialty? The answer tells you everything.
- **Ask about their Texas medical license support.** Texas has its own licensing quirks — the TMB's Locum Tenens registration is separate from a full license, and the processing timeline can surprise administrators who've only worked in other states. Good agencies have a licensing coordinator who lives in this weeds.
- **Nail down the guarantee policy in writing.** Top-tier agencies offer a placement guarantee — if the provider doesn't work out within the first week or two, they replace them at no additional cost. Get the exact terms, not a verbal reassurance.
- **Require malpractice documentation before the first shift.** Most agencies carry occurrence-based or claims-made coverage; know which one you're getting, what the per-occurrence limits are, and whether tail coverage is included. In Texas, this matters — don't sign off on a start date until you have the certificate in hand.

> **Pro Tip:** Dallas-[Fort Worth](/locations/texas/fort-worth/) is served by multiple regional offices of national agencies. Ask whether your account will be managed locally or routed through a national call center — response time when you have a last-minute gap is night and day between the two.

## What to Expect

Locum tenens contracts in Dallas typically run $20,000–$150,000 depending on specialty, duration, and call burden — hospitalist and EM coverage on the lower end, surgical subspecialties and rural-adjacent placements pushing the ceiling. Most agencies quote an all-in hourly or daily rate that includes their markup, malpractice, and travel logistics, but "all-in" can mean different things depending on who's handling hotel and per diem.

> **Reality Check:** The most common pricing mistake administrators make is comparing agencies on quoted rate alone without accounting for travel cost handling. An agency quoting $180/hour and covering travel can be cheaper than one quoting $165/hour where you're booking flights and hotels separately. Get the total cost of engagement, not just the hourly.

Credentialing timelines for straightforward placements typically run 2–4 weeks from signed agreement to first shift; complex situations (multiple state licenses, hospital-specific privileging requirements) can stretch to 6–8 weeks. Emergency coverage is possible faster if the provider is already credentialed at your facility.

## Local Market Overview

Dallas sits at the intersection of three major health system footprints — UT Southwestern, Baylor Scott & White, and HCA's North Texas division — which means locum demand is consistently high and good providers book fast. The DFW metroplex also draws providers who want urban amenities without coastal costs, giving well-connected agencies a genuine recruiting advantage over firms that treat Texas as a secondary market.
