## How to Choose a Locum Tenens Agency in Tampa

- **Verify NALTO membership and Joint Commission certification first.** These aren't vanity badges — they signal the agency operates under a code of ethics and has passed third-party credentialing audits. An uncredentialed agency that cuts corners on primary source verification is your liability problem the moment something goes wrong at the bedside.
- **Ask specifically about Florida licensure speed.** Florida's DOH has improved but still runs 6–10 weeks for full licensure. Top agencies maintain a bench of Florida-licensed physicians ready to deploy; ask what percentage of their current Tampa-area candidates hold active Florida licenses before you discuss rates.
- **Probe their specialty depth, not just their roster size.** A 50,000-provider database means nothing if you need a locum hospitalist with nocturnist experience and you're getting pitched a semi-retired internist. Push for placement counts in your specific specialty within the last 90 days.
- **Get the guarantee policy in writing, upfront.** If a placed physician walks after two weeks, what happens? The best agencies offer a prorated refund or a no-cost replacement within a defined window. Vague language here is a red flag.
- **Check malpractice tail coverage explicitly.** Florida is a claims-made state with an active malpractice environment. Confirm whether the agency provides occurrence-form coverage or claims-made with guaranteed tail — and get the certificate before the physician's first shift, not after.

> **Pro Tip:** Florida has a Physician Workforce Annual Report published by the State Surgeon General's office. Cross-reference it against an agency's claimed specialty depth for Tampa Bay — if they say they place 40 emergency medicine physicians a year in Florida but the statewide data shows tight EM supply, ask how.

## What to Expect

Locum tenens contracts in Tampa typically run $20,000–$150,000 depending on specialty, duration, and urgency — hospitalists and primary care sit at the lower end; subspecialists (neurosurgery, interventional cardiology, neonatology) push the ceiling hard. Agencies charge an all-in bill rate that bundles the physician's pay, benefits, malpractice, licensing fees, and travel; expect 30–45% above the physician's take-home as a rough margin, though negotiation on volume and duration is real. Standard time-to-fill on a credentialed, Florida-licensed candidate runs 2–4 weeks for planned coverage and 48–72 hours for true emergency placements.

> **Reality Check:** Administrators frequently anchor on bill rate as the sole cost metric and undercount the internal staff hours spent onboarding, scheduling, and managing a poorly matched locum. A slightly higher rate from an agency with a rigorous fit process and a proactive account manager almost always costs less in the end than a bargain placement that turns into a management headache.
