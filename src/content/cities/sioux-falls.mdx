## How to Choose a Locum Tenens Agency in Sioux Falls

- **Verify NALTO membership first.** NALTO members are bound to a code of ethics that covers transparency in billing, honest candidate representation, and clear contract terms. In South Dakota's tight-knit medical community, an agency that plays games will get known for it fast — but you don't want to be the one who finds out firsthand.
- **Ask specifically about South Dakota licensing timelines.** SD uses the Interstate Medical Licensure Compact, which can cut licensing from months to weeks for eligible physicians. A good agency will already know this and will have a process built around it. If they seem surprised when you mention IMLC, hang up.
- **Demand primary source verification documentation upfront.** Not a summary — the actual credentials file. NCQA-accredited agencies maintain structured credential packets; agencies that hedge on this are usually working from incomplete or stale records.
- **Check their specialty depth in rural-adjacent markets.** Sioux Falls serves as the regional referral hub for a multi-state catchment area stretching into Iowa, Nebraska, and Minnesota. An agency that only places in urban academic centers will struggle to find candidates willing to cover the case mix — and volume — you're actually running.
- **Clarify the malpractice tail policy before you sign anything.** Most reputable agencies provide occurrence-based or tail-covered claims-made policies. If an agency is vague about who pays the tail when an engagement ends, that's a dealbreaker.

> **Pro Tip:** Ask every agency for their average time-to-placement for your specific specialty in markets comparable to Sioux Falls. "We can usually fill in 2–3 weeks" is marketing. "For hospitalists in Midwest mid-sized systems, our median fill time is 11 days" is a data point you can use.

## What to Expect

Locum tenens contracts in Sioux Falls range from roughly $20,000 for a single weekend shift block up to $150,000 or more for multi-month specialty placements — the agency markup is typically 25–40% above the physician's daily rate, baked into what you're quoted. Full-service agencies bundle travel, housing, licensing, and malpractice into that number; discount shops sometimes unbundle and hit you with line items later.

> **Reality Check:** The most common budget mistake administrators make is comparing agency quotes without confirming what's included. One quote with housing and travel can look $15,000 more expensive than a bare-bones quote that will actually cost $8,000 more once you add those back. Get itemized breakdowns, not totals.

Expect 1–3 weeks from intake call to first candidate presentation for common specialties; subspecialties with thin national supply (pediatric neurology, interventional cardiology) can run 3–6 weeks even with a good agency working hard.
