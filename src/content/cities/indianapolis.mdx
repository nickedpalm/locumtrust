## How to Choose a Locum Tenens Agency in Indianapolis

- **Verify NALTO membership and credentialing accreditation before anything else.** NALTO members are bound by a code of ethics that covers disclosure, fee transparency, and candidate representation. Pair that with NCQA or URAC accreditation and you have a credentialing process with external accountability — not just an agency's word that primary source verification got done.
- **Ask specifically about Indiana medical licensing timelines.** The Indiana Professional Licensing Agency runs on its own clock. Agencies with an active Indiana roster can often place a locum within 2–3 weeks; agencies that are sourcing new candidates for the state may need 6–8 weeks just for licensure. Know which situation you're in before you sign.
- **Get the malpractice coverage terms in writing, including tail.** Most reputable agencies provide occurrence-based coverage, but some offer claims-made policies — which means if a claim surfaces after the engagement ends, you may be holding the bag. Confirm who pays tail coverage before the contract is executed.
- **Ask about specialty depth, not just headcount.** An agency with 10,000 providers on paper but only 40 in hospitalist or emergency medicine is useless if that's your gap. Indianapolis's hospital market — IU Health Methodist, Community Health Network, Franciscan — skews heavily toward procedural specialties. Ask your rep for a concrete number of credentialed, available candidates in your specific specialty.
- **Check the guarantee policy.** Top-tier agencies will replace a locum who doesn't work out within the first week or two, no questions asked. If an agency won't put that in writing, treat it as a red flag.

> **Pro Tip:** Indianapolis is a major academic medical center hub — which means IU School of Medicine has a steady pipeline of residents and fellows who've trained here and often want to stay in the region for locum work. Ask agencies specifically whether they have Indiana-trained physicians on their bench. Familiarity with local EMR systems (Epic is near-universal here) and institutional culture cuts onboarding friction significantly.

## What to Expect

Locum tenens contracts in Indianapolis typically run $20,000–$150,000 depending on specialty, duration, and shift intensity — with emergency medicine, anesthesiology, and surgical subspecialties sitting at the higher end of that range. Credentialing-to-first-shift timelines average 3–6 weeks for agencies with established Indiana rosters, longer if licensing is starting from scratch. Agency fees are baked into the bill rate, so you won't see a separate line item, but you should ask for a full bill rate breakdown so you can benchmark against market.

> **Reality Check:** The most common pricing mistake administrators make is comparing agencies on bill rate alone without accounting for the all-in cost of a bad placement — lost revenue from a shift that still needs coverage, credentialing rework, and staff disruption. A $15/hour cheaper bill rate evaporates fast if the agency's guarantee policy is weak or their candidate screening is thin.

## Local Market Overview

Indianapolis sits at the intersection of a dense academic medical ecosystem and a high-demand rural coverage corridor — Marion County facilities compete for the same locum pool as underserved systems in southern Indiana, which drives up placement timelines and premium rates for high-demand specialties during peak periods. The city's concentration of large health systems also means agencies with existing MSA relationships at IU Health or Community Health Network can often fast-track facility credentialing, which is the bottleneck most administrators don't account for until it's already costing them shifts.
