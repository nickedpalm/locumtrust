Finding a reliable locum tenens agency in [Los Angeles](/locations/california/los-angeles/) shouldn't feel like a second job — but for most CMOs and administrators, it does. The LA healthcare market is sprawling, hyper-competitive, and unforgiving: you're working against a pool of 100+ agencies, a credentialing ecosystem that moves at its own pace, and the constant pressure of California's unique licensing requirements. This directory cuts through the noise and lists only agencies with verified credentials, real specialty coverage, and a track record in the Southern California market.

## How to Choose a Locum Tenens Agency in Los Angeles

- **Verify NALTO membership before any other step.** NALTO members are bound by a code of ethics that prohibits fee-splitting and bait-and-switch billing. In a market as busy as LA, fly-by-night shops are a real risk.
- **Ask specifically about California Medical Board processing times.** California licensing runs notoriously slower than most states — a good agency will tell you the real timeline (often 60–90 days for out-of-state physicians) upfront, not after you've already committed.
- **Check for Joint Commission Certified Staffing Organization status.** In LA's hospital networks — Cedars-Sinai, UCLA Health, Kaiser Southern California — this certification is increasingly a vendor requirement, not a nice-to-have.
- **Confirm malpractice coverage structure in writing.** Most agencies offer claims-made coverage; you want occurrence-based or a solid tail policy. Ask who pays the tail. Get it in the contract.
- **Demand a specialty-specific roster, not a generic one.** LA has genuine depth needs in hospitalist medicine, emergency, and behavioral health. Any agency worth your time should be able to show you placed physicians in your specific specialty in the last 90 days.

> **Pro Tip:** Call the agency's references on a weekday morning — not email, phone. Administrators who've actually used a service will tell you things in 3 minutes that no written testimonial ever will. Ask: "Did they hit their credentialing timeline?" and "What happened when something went wrong?"

## What to Expect

Locum tenens contracts in the LA market typically run $20,000–$150,000 depending on specialty, contract length, and coverage intensity — emergency and surgical subspecialties sit at the high end, while primary care and urgent care skew lower. Most agencies quote an all-in bill rate that bundles the physician's compensation, travel, housing, and their margin; expect 20–35% agency overhead baked into that number. Credentialing and privileging will be your longest lead time — budget 6–8 weeks minimum for a new-to-California physician placement.

> **Reality Check:** The most common budget mistake isn't overpaying the agency — it's underestimating the downstream cost of a slow credentialing process. A physician who can't see patients because hospital privileging stalled is a $10,000–$15,000/week problem. Agencies that offer guaranteed start dates backed by a penalty clause are worth paying a small premium for.

## Local Market Overview

Los Angeles operates under California's some of the strictest healthcare staffing regulations in the country — including AB 5 considerations that can affect how locum arrangements are structured, and a Department of Managed Health Care that moves slowly on plan-side authorizations. Agencies that have placed staff in LA's large integrated systems (Kaiser, [Providence](/locations/rhode-island/providence/), UCLA) before understand these friction points; agencies that don't will learn on your timeline.
