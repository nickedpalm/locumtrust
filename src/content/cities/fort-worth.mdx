## How to Choose a Locum Tenens Agency in Fort Worth

- **Verify NALTO membership before you have a serious conversation.** NALTO members are bound by a code of ethics covering fee transparency and provider disclosure. Non-members aren't. In a market as active as Fort Worth — where JPS Health Network, Texas Health Resources, and Cook Children's are all competing for the same pool of temporary hospitalists and surgical subspecialists — unscrupulous shops have plenty of cover.
- **Ask specifically about Texas licensure turnaround.** The Texas Medical Board is not the fastest board in the country. Top agencies maintain pre-licensed provider benches or have documented relationships with expedited licensing services. If a recruiter can't give you a realistic timeline for your specialty, that's your answer.
- **Confirm malpractice coverage structure in writing.** Most agencies carry occurrence-based coverage, but some offer claims-made policies that leave you exposed after the engagement ends. Get the certificate. Review the limits. $1M/$3M is a common floor; some specialties (OB, neurosurgery) warrant more.
- **Probe their credentialing speed with a real scenario.** Ask: "How fast can you get me a credentialed emergency medicine physician for a 13-week contract starting in 30 days?" A quality agency with Joint Commission Certified Staffing Organization or NCQA Credentialing Accreditation status should be able to walk you through exactly how they'd execute that. Vague answers mean manual, slow processes.
- **Check their guarantee policy.** If a placed provider leaves mid-contract or doesn't meet performance expectations, what happens? Free replacement within X days? Partial refund? Silence on this question is a red flag.

> **Pro Tip:** Fort Worth's hospital market skews heavily toward employed-physician models, which means locum coverage gaps often hit harder and faster than in markets with more independent practitioners. Ask agencies whether they've placed in Tarrant County specifically — familiarity with local credentialing contacts at JPS or Texas Health Harris Methodist can shave days off your onboarding timeline.

## What to Expect

Locum tenens contracts in Fort Worth typically run $20,000–$150,000 depending on specialty, contract length, and urgency — emergency medicine and surgical subspecialties sit at the high end, while primary care and hospitalist coverage trend lower. Most agencies work on a spread model (the difference between what you pay and what the provider receives), so you won't always see a fee line on your invoice. Turnaround from first contact to credentialed provider on-site averages 4–8 weeks for planned coverage; crisis placements can move faster but expect a premium.

> **Reality Check:** The most common mistake administrators make is negotiating only on the weekly rate without locking in an all-in total cost estimate. Travel, housing, and malpractice coverage are often line items that surface after you've already committed. Get a written total cost projection before signing anything.
