## How to Choose a Locum Tenens Agency in Phoenix

- **Verify NALTO membership independently.** Don't take an agency's word for it. NALTO's member directory is public. Members are bound by a code of ethics that includes honest fee disclosure and no double-booking — both problems that surface regularly in unaffiliated agencies.
- **Ask specifically about Arizona licensing timelines.** The Arizona Medical Board has improved processing speeds, but multi-state compact participation (for APPs) and J-1 waiver positions in underserved areas add complexity. A competent agency will quote you realistic timelines by specialty, not a generic "2–4 weeks."
- **Probe their specialty depth, not their specialty breadth.** Any agency will claim they cover 40 specialties. Ask how many hospitalist placements they've made in the Phoenix metro in the last 12 months and what their fill rate was. Vague answers mean a thin bench.
- **Request their malpractice coverage terms in writing upfront.** Most agencies provide occurrence-based coverage, but the limits and tail coverage details vary significantly. For high-acuity specialties — ED, OB, anesthesia — this is non-negotiable to clarify before any engagement.
- **Understand their guarantee policy before the provider shows up.** If a placed physician leaves after week one, what happens? Top agencies offer replacement guarantees or fee credits. Know the terms before you're in a bind.

> **Pro Tip:** Phoenix's seasonality is real. Winter months bring a surge in snowbird-driven patient volume that hits hospitalist and orthopedic demand hard. If you're planning coverage for November through March, engage agencies 60–90 days out rather than the standard 30 — fill rates tighten significantly in Q4.

## What to Expect

Locum tenens contracts in the Phoenix market typically run $20,000–$150,000 per engagement depending on specialty, duration, and whether you need an MD or an APP — emergency medicine and anesthesia sit at the high end; urgent care and family medicine coverage is more accessible. The process generally runs: needs assessment and specialty brief, agency candidate sourcing (1–2 weeks for common specialties, 3–4 for surgical subspecialties), credentialing and privileging (your facility drives this timeline), and onboarding logistics.

> **Reality Check:** The most common pricing mistake is treating the agency fee as the only cost. Factor in housing stipends, travel, and malpractice coverage — which the agency pays but which is priced into the bill rate. Ask for a fully-loaded rate comparison across at least two agencies, because markup structures vary enough to matter on a multi-month contract.

## Local Market Overview

Phoenix operates in one of the country's fastest-growing healthcare markets — Banner Health alone runs 12 facilities in the metro, and the Valley's rapid expansion into suburbs like Gilbert, Chandler, and Surprise has created persistent coverage gaps in primary care and behavioral health that locum agencies have been filling for years. Arizona's participation in the Nurse Licensure Compact (NLC) and its expedited locum licensing pathways for physicians make the regulatory environment more favorable than many states, but rural coverage gaps in Yuma and Flagstaff (often staffed from the Phoenix pool) add complexity that separates agencies with genuine Arizona experience from those just running national playbooks.
