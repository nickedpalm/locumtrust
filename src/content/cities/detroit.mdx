Finding a locum tenens agency in [Detroit](/locations/michigan/detroit/) that can actually deliver — credentialed, malpractice-covered, and on-site before your census takes a hit — is harder than the agency websites make it look. Southeast Michigan's healthcare market is dense with regional health systems competing for the same pool of temporary physicians, and the agencies serving it range from NALTO-certified operations with deep specialty benches to staffing mills that'll send you a CV and disappear. This directory exists to cut through the noise.

## How to Choose a Locum Tenens Agency in Detroit

- **Verify NALTO membership before anything else.** NALTO members are bound to a code of ethics that covers honest CV representation, transparent fee disclosure, and no candidate poaching from your permanent staff. Non-members aren't. In a market where Henry Ford Health, Detroit Medical Center, and Corewell Health are all competing for the same locum physicians, you cannot afford to work with an agency that plays loose with credential verification.
- **Ask specifically about Michigan licensure timelines.** Michigan's Board of Medicine processes temporary licenses, but turnaround varies significantly by specialty. An agency that places regularly in the state should know the current processing window and have a track record of managing expedited applications — not just promise you "we handle it."
- **Check their specialty depth, not just their roster size.** A 50,000-provider database means nothing if half of them are primary care and you need a nocturnist or an interventional cardiologist. Ask for placement counts by specialty in Michigan over the last 12 months.
- **Require a written guarantee policy.** Top-tier agencies offer replacement guarantees if a placed provider leaves early. Get the terms in writing — specifically what triggers coverage, the replacement window, and whether the agency absorbs any rebooking fees.
- **Confirm malpractice coverage structure upfront.** Most agencies provide occurrence-based or claims-made policies. Know which you're getting, and confirm tail coverage is included if it's claims-made. Don't let this be a post-placement surprise.

> **Pro Tip:** Detroit's major health systems often have preferred vendor agreements that route locum requests through specific agencies. If you're at an independent practice or smaller clinic, you're not bound by those agreements — which means you can negotiate harder on placement fees and guarantee terms than a large system typically can.

## What to Expect

Locum tenens contracts in this market typically run $20,000–$150,000 total depending on specialty, duration, and whether the engagement covers overnight or weekend shifts — EM and surgical subspecialties anchor the high end, while primary care and general internal medicine sit closer to the floor. Most agencies quote an all-in blended rate that covers physician compensation, malpractice, and travel logistics, though credentialing fees are sometimes itemized separately.

> **Reality Check:** The agencies quoting you the lowest bill rate are usually cutting corners on credentialing thoroughness or malpractice limits — not running an efficient operation. A $10/hour difference in bill rate that comes with a 45-day credentialing process or a $1M/$3M policy instead of $1M/$3M per-occurrence can cost you far more in operational disruption or liability exposure than you saved.

## Local Market Overview

Detroit's healthcare infrastructure is anchored by large, academically affiliated systems — Wayne State partnerships, Children's Hospital of Michigan, and a dense network of FQHCs serving the city's underserved neighborhoods — which means locum demand here skews toward specialists and providers comfortable navigating complex, high-acuity patient populations. Agencies with existing relationships in urban academic medicine, particularly those that have placed in Great Lakes regional hospitals, will credential and onboard faster than generalist staffing firms parachuting into the Michigan market for the first time.
