## How to Choose a Locum Tenens Agency in Pittsburgh

- **Verify NALTO membership before anything else.** NALTO members are bound to a code of ethics that covers fee transparency, credentialing standards, and non-compete practices. In a market where some agencies double-bill facilities and providers simultaneously, this matters more than their website claims.
- **Ask specifically about Pennsylvania licensure turnaround.** PA is not a compact state for physicians (though it joined the Interstate Medical Licensure Compact), so a new-to-state provider can add 4–8 weeks to your timeline. Good agencies have dedicated licensing coordinators who know Pennsylvania's Board of Medicine quirks and can shave weeks off that window.
- **Match specialty depth to your actual gap.** A generalist agency that staffs everything from EM to dermatology often means a thinner bench in any single specialty. If you're covering a hospitalist vacancy at a community hospital in the Pittsburgh suburbs, ask how many hospitalists they've placed in Western PA in the last 12 months — not nationally.
- **Get the credentialing protocol in writing.** Joint Commission Certified agencies and NCQA-credentialed shops follow primary source verification standards. Ask for their average credentialing time and whether they carry tail coverage. Agencies that hedge on this are a liability, not a solution.
- **Check for local market relationships.** Agencies with a Pittsburgh-area track record know the informal stuff — which facilities have credentialing backlogs, which neighborhoods affect housing logistics for traveling providers, which systems have specific EMR requirements. That institutional knowledge is worth paying for.

> **Pro Tip:** Ask the agency what their "fallback" looks like if their placed provider cancels within 72 hours. Top-tier agencies have bench depth and guarantee policies. Everyone else has excuses.

## What to Expect

Locum tenens contracts in Pittsburgh typically run $20,000–$150,000 depending on specialty, duration, and urgency — hospitalists and primary care sit at the lower end, surgical subspecialties and proceduralists at the top. Most agencies charge the facility directly via a markup on the provider's daily rate, so you're not writing two checks. Expect a 2–4 week standard timeline from intake call to credentialed provider on-site, though agencies with pre-credentialed Pittsburgh-area physicians can move faster.

> **Reality Check:** The biggest pricing mistake facilities make is comparing agencies on day-rate alone without accounting for what's included. Some bundle housing, travel, and malpractice tail coverage. Others itemize everything and the "cheaper" agency ends up costing 15% more once you run the full picture. Get a total cost-per-engagement quote, not just the provider day rate.

## Local Market Overview

Pittsburgh's healthcare landscape is dominated by two massive systems — UPMC and AHN — but the surrounding regional hospitals (WVU Medicine, Excela Health, Heritage Valley) are where locum demand is most acute and competition for qualified agencies is genuinely fierce. The region's aging population and rural catchment areas mean chronic specialist shortages in cardiology, nephrology, and orthopedics, which makes specialty-deep agencies with a strong Western Pennsylvania placement history worth the extra vetting time.
