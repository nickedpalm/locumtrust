Finding a qualified locum tenens agency in [Boise](/locations/idaho/boise/) shouldn't require a phone tree and three LinkedIn cold messages — but here we are. Idaho's healthcare landscape is tighter than most administrators expect: St. Luke's and Saint Alphonsus dominate the Treasure Valley, rural access gaps stretch into the Magic Valley, and physician shortages run deeper than the national average. When your hospitalist team is down a body or your urgent care is facing a six-week vacancy, the agency you call in the next 48 hours matters more than the one you vetted last quarter.

## How to Choose a Locum Tenens Agency in Boise

- **Verify NALTO membership and credentialing accreditation first.** NALTO members adhere to a code of ethics and industry standards. NCQA or Joint Commission credentialing accreditation means primary source verification is actually happening — not just a checkbox exercise.
- **Ask specifically about Idaho licensing timelines.** Idaho Medical Board processing runs 4–8 weeks for standard applications. Any agency promising faster placement without a clear locum license or Interstate Medical Licensure Compact (IMLC) pathway is either cutting corners or hasn't done it here before.
- **Match specialty depth to your gap.** A generalist agency might cover your ER shift, but for subspecialties — nephrology, interventional cardiology, maternal-fetal medicine — you want an agency whose bench in that specialty is measured in hundreds, not dozens.
- **Get the guarantee policy in writing before you sign.** Best-in-class agencies offer a free replacement within 30 days if a placement doesn't work out. Many don't. Know which kind you're dealing with before your CMO is fielding complaints.
- **Check malpractice coverage terms.** Occurrence-based coverage protects you after the engagement ends; claims-made does not (without a tail). In Idaho, where rural facilities sometimes host locums for short stints with delayed incident reporting, this distinction is not hypothetical.

> **Pro Tip:** Boise's growth has made it a more competitive placement market than it was five years ago. Agencies with existing relationships at St. Luke's Health System or Saint Alphonsus Regional Medical Center will onboard faster — ask them directly if they've placed there before and get a reference.

## What to Expect

Locum tenens contracts in Boise typically run $20,000–$150,000 depending on specialty, duration, and urgency — hospitalists and family medicine sit toward the lower end, procedural specialists and surgical subspecialties command the top of that range. Most agencies quote an all-in bill rate that includes the physician's compensation, malpractice, travel, and housing; credentialing adds 2–6 weeks to your lead time if the provider isn't already licensed in Idaho.

> **Reality Check:** The most common pricing mistake isn't overpaying — it's underestimating total cost by comparing agency bill rates to a staff physician's base salary. The bill rate covers malpractice, housing, travel, and recruiter overhead. Compare it to your total cost-per-shift for a fully loaded employed physician before deciding it's expensive.

The Treasure Valley's physician-to-population ratio has lagged migration growth for years, which means the locum tenens market here is a seller's market — experienced providers have options, and agencies with strong provider relationships will get first call. If you're planning a multi-month engagement or building out a new service line at a community clinic, locking in a reliable agency before the gap opens is worth more than any last-minute scramble.
