## How to Choose a Locum Tenens Agency in Little Rock

- **Verify NALTO membership first, not last.** NALTO members are bound to a code of ethics that covers fee transparency and credentialing timelines. In a market where informal broker networks still operate, this single filter removes a lot of headaches before they start.
- **Ask specifically about Arkansas licensure support.** The Arkansas State Medical Board moves at its own pace. Agencies that place regularly in the state will have a dedicated licensing coordinator who knows the board's quirks — expect them to give you a realistic timeline (typically 6–10 weeks for a new Arkansas license) without being asked.
- **Match specialty depth to your need.** A generalist agency with 40,000 providers on their roster sounds impressive until you need a pediatric hospitalist in Pulaski County on three weeks' notice. Ask how many placements they've made in your specialty in Arkansas in the last 12 months. Single digits is a red flag.
- **Clarify malpractice coverage structure upfront.** Claims-made vs. occurrence-based policies affect your facility's exposure after the engagement ends. Reputable agencies carry occurrence-based coverage or provide tail — confirm this in writing before the first CV lands in your inbox.
- **Check Joint Commission or NCQA credentialing accreditation.** These aren't vanity credentials. They signal that primary source verification is process-driven, not person-dependent — which matters when your compliance team is doing the post-placement audit.

> **Pro Tip:** Little Rock's proximity to [Memphis](/locations/tennessee/memphis/) and [Dallas](/locations/texas/dallas/) means some agencies will pitch you providers who are actually based three states away and billing travel premiums into the rate. Ask for the provider's current state of residence before the placement conversation goes further.

## What to Expect

Locum tenens contracts in Little Rock typically run $20,000–$150,000 depending on specialty, duration, and urgency — ER and anesthesia placements at the high end, primary care coverage on the lower end of the range. Most credentialed agencies can have a qualified CV in your hands within 48–72 hours of an intake call; privileging at your facility is usually the longer pole in the tent.

> **Reality Check:** The most common budget mistake administrators make is quoting the daily physician rate without modeling the full agency fee — which typically runs 15–25% on top. If an agency quote looks suspiciously low, ask directly whether the management fee is included or billed separately. It usually isn't.

Little Rock's healthcare landscape is anchored by UAMS Medical Center, CHI St. Vincent, and Baptist Health, which means most locum demand flows through large systems with credentialing committees that have seen enough temporary coverage requests to have strong vendor preferences.
