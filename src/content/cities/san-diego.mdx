Finding a qualified locum tenens agency in [San Diego](/locations/california/san-diego/) shouldn't feel like navigating the I-5 at rush hour — but for most CMOs and clinic administrators, it does. The San Diego market runs hot: UCSD Health, Scripps, Sharp, and a dense network of military-adjacent clinics create constant demand for temporary coverage, and the agencies that actually deliver are buried under a pile of ones that just promise they can. This directory exists to cut through that noise.

## How to Choose a Locum Tenens Agency in San Diego

- **Verify NALTO membership before anything else.** NALTO members are bound to a code of ethics that covers fee transparency, provider vetting, and contract terms. Non-members have no accountability structure — and there are plenty of them calling San Diego hospital systems every week.
- **Ask specifically about California licensing turnaround.** The Medical Board of California is notoriously slow. A credentialing team that doesn't have experience expediting CA licenses will cost you weeks you don't have. Ask for their average California licensing timeline — if they can't answer in under 30 seconds, that tells you something.
- **Confirm primary source verification is in-house, not outsourced.** Some agencies farm credentialing out to third parties and mark it up. You want Joint Commission Certified or NCQA-accredited credentialing done under one roof, with a dedicated verifier who can answer your questions directly.
- **Check specialty depth, not just specialty breadth.** Any agency can claim to place hospitalists. The ones worth hiring have active, credentialed hospitalists in Southern California ready to deploy — not a national database of candidates they haven't spoken to in eight months.
- **Get the guarantee policy in writing.** What happens if a placed provider leaves mid-contract? The top-tier agencies absorb the replacement cost. The others will send you a new invoice.

> **Pro Tip:** San Diego's proximity to the border means bilingual (English/Spanish) providers are genuinely valued — especially for community clinics in National City, Chula Vista, and the South Bay. Ask agencies specifically whether they track language capability in their candidate profiles. The ones that do have thought harder about this market than the ones that don't.

## What to Expect

Locum tenens contracts in San Diego typically run $20,000–$150,000 depending on specialty, duration, and urgency — with EM, anesthesia, and surgical subspecialties at the top of that range and primary care and urgent care closer to the floor. Most reputable agencies can have credentialed candidates in front of you within 72 hours for an initial call, with full placement in two to six weeks depending on how complex your credentialing requirements are.

> **Reality Check:** The biggest pricing mistake administrators make is comparing agency fees without accounting for what's bundled. Malpractice coverage, travel, housing, and licensing fees can add 20–30% to the sticker rate if they're not included. Always ask for an all-in cost-per-day estimate before comparing quotes across agencies — the cheapest headline number often isn't.

## Local Market Overview

San Diego's healthcare landscape is unusually fragmented for a city its size — a mix of large academic systems, military treatment facilities transitioning patients to civilian networks, and a sprawling federally-qualified health center system serving the county's substantial uninsured population. That fragmentation means demand for locum coverage spikes unpredictably, and agencies with existing relationships and credentialed providers already active in the California market will always outperform national shops trying to work the state from scratch.
