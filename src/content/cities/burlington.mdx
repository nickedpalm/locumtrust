Finding the hospital staffing manager who just got a two-week notice from their only hospitalist and is now staring down an unscheduled Sunday shift with no backup is the exact scenario the Vermont locum tenens market was built for — and the agencies listed here have filled it.

## How to Choose a Locum Tenens Agency in [Burlington](/locations/vermont/burlington/)

- **Verify NALTO membership first.** NALTO members are bound by a code of ethics that prohibits double-booking and requires transparent fee disclosure. In a small market like Burlington, where the same physician might get shopped by three agencies simultaneously, this matters more than it does in [Boston](/locations/massachusetts/boston/).
- **Ask specifically about Vermont licensure timelines.** Vermont's Board of Medical Practice can run 6–10 weeks for standard licensure. Agencies with existing relationships at the Board — or that carry physicians already holding VT licenses — cut that window dramatically. If an agency quotes you less than four weeks without a licensed-in-state candidate already on hand, push back.
- **Check credentialing depth against UVM Medical Center standards.** Fletcher Allen/UVM Health Network is the dominant referral hub in the region. Agencies that have placed at UVM before will have credentialing packets pre-formatted to their committee's specifications. That alone can shave two to three weeks off your timeline.
- **Match the agency's specialty bench to your actual gap.** Some agencies run deep in primary care and hospitalist coverage but thin in subspecialties. Burlington's tertiary referral volume means you may need surgical subspecialists — confirm the agency has placed in your exact specialty, not just adjacent ones.
- **Get the guarantee policy in writing before you sign.** If a physician leaves mid-engagement, who covers the gap? The best agencies offer a free replacement or prorated refund. Vague language here is a red flag.

> **Pro Tip:** Vermont has a reciprocal agreement with several neighboring states, so physicians licensed in New Hampshire or [New York](/locations/new-york/new-york/) can sometimes obtain expedited VT licensure. Ask your agency directly whether they have multi-state licensed candidates on their active roster — this is the fastest path to coverage.

## What to Expect

Locum tenens contracts in Burlington typically run $20,000–$150,000 depending on specialty, duration, and whether travel and housing are bundled — hospitalist weekend coverage sits toward the lower end, while subspecialist multi-month engagements push the ceiling. The agency's fee (typically 25–35% of physician compensation) is baked into that range, not added on top. Most credentialed candidates can be presented within 48–72 hours of an intake call; placement — meaning credentialed, oriented, and on-shift — takes two to six weeks depending on the facility's own privileging timeline, not just the agency's speed.

> **Reality Check:** The most common pricing mistake is comparing agency rates without accounting for what's included. A lower agency fee that excludes malpractice tail coverage can cost you significantly more than a higher-fee agency that bundles it. Always ask: "Who carries the malpractice, and does it include tail?"

## Local Market Overview

Burlington is a mid-size academic medical hub punching above its weight — UVM Medical Center draws referrals from across Vermont, northern New Hampshire, and upstate New York, which means locum demand concentrates in subspecialties and inpatient coverage rather than routine primary care. The city's size also means physician community overlap is real: a physician who had a poor experience at your facility may be placed by a different agency six months later, so treating locum providers well is a retention strategy as much as a coverage one.
