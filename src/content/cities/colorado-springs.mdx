Finding a qualified locum tenens agency in [Colorado Springs](/locations/colorado/colorado-springs/) shouldn't feel like a procurement nightmare — but between the agencies that overpromise credentialing speed and the ones that quietly lack malpractice coverage continuity, hospital CMOs and clinic administrators in the Pikes Peak region get burned more often than they should. This directory exists so you don't have to sort through the noise yourself.

## How to Choose a Locum Tenens Agency in Colorado Springs

- **Verify NALTO membership before anything else.** NALTO members are bound to a code of ethics that covers fee transparency, candidate representation, and non-compete restrictions. Non-member agencies aren't held to those standards — and in a market where UCHealth Memorial and Penrose-St. Francis are both competing for the same thin pool of specialists, that distinction matters.
- **Ask specifically about Colorado licensing turnaround.** Colorado is a Compact state for physicians (IMLC) and for NPs/PAs, which means a good agency should be placing credentialed providers within 2–4 weeks for compact-eligible candidates — not 6–8. If they hedge on this, they don't know the compact rules well enough.
- **Confirm malpractice tail coverage in writing.** Some agencies provide occurrence-based coverage; others provide claims-made with a tail you're responsible for. Get the certificate of insurance before the provider sets foot in your facility.
- **Push on specialty depth, not headcount.** An agency with 30,000 providers in a database is meaningless if 28,000 are family medicine generalists. Ask how many credentialed hospitalists, intensivists, or your specific needed specialty they placed in the Mountain West in the last 12 months.
- **Understand their guarantee policy.** Top-tier agencies offer a replacement guarantee if a provider leaves mid-contract. Get the terms in writing — specifically what triggers it and what the turnaround timeline is.

> **Pro Tip:** Colorado Springs has a military-heavy population (Fort Carson, Peterson SFB, Schriever SFB), which means behavioral health, orthopedics, and TBI specialists are in chronic undersupply at civilian facilities. Lead with your specialty need upfront — agencies that work this market know which providers are already licensed in-state.

## What to Expect

Locum tenens contracts in Colorado Springs typically run $20,000–$150,000 depending on specialty, duration, and whether housing and travel are bundled — hospitalists and surgeons sit at the higher end, with primary care filling the lower range. Most agencies work on a markup model (30–50% above the provider's bill rate), though you're rarely shown that math directly. Placement from first call to first shift runs 2–6 weeks for compact-eligible providers and 6–12 weeks if the physician needs a new Colorado license from scratch.

> **Reality Check:** The single most common pricing mistake administrators make is comparing total contract cost without accounting for what's included. Agency A quotes $180/hour all-in (malpractice, travel, housing). Agency B quotes $155/hour and invoices travel and lodging separately. Agency B is more expensive. Always ask for a fully-loaded rate and get housing and travel treatment spelled out in the contract.
