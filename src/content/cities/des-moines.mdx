## How to Choose a Locum Tenens Agency in Des Moines

- **Verify NALTO membership first, ask about Iowa licensure speed second.** NALTO members are bound by a code of ethics that covers fee transparency and credentialing standards. But Iowa's medical license timeline runs 6–10 weeks for most specialties — your agency needs a documented track record of managing that clock, not just a promise to "expedite."
- **Match the agency to your specialty gap, not just your timeline.** An agency that dominates hospitalist placements may have a thin bench for rural-adjacent specialties like general surgery or OB/GYN — both of which see chronic shortages in the Des Moines metro and surrounding I-80 corridor communities.
- **Ask for their credentialing SLA in writing.** Top-tier agencies with NCQA accreditation or Joint Commission certification can typically complete primary source verification in 5–10 business days. If an agency can't give you a number, that's your number.
- **Clarify the guarantee policy before you sign.** If a placed provider leaves within 30 days, what happens? Some agencies offer full replacement at no additional cost; others prorate. The difference matters a lot on a $60,000 contract.
- **Get the malpractice structure in writing.** Most reputable agencies carry occurrence-based coverage (protects against claims filed after the assignment ends). Claims-made coverage leaves you exposed if a patient files six months later.

> **Pro Tip:** Iowa Health System and UnityPoint Health dominate the Des Moines market, and many agencies have established credentialing relationships with their medical staff offices. Ask specifically whether the agency has placed providers at your facility type before — the difference between a first placement and a fifth is measurable in days.

## What to Expect

Locum tenens contracts in Des Moines typically run $20,000–$150,000 depending on specialty, duration, and shift intensity — primary care and family medicine sit toward the lower end, while proceduralists and subspecialists command premium rates. Most placements take 2–6 weeks from initial request to first shift, though urgent coverage windows can compress that to under two weeks with the right agency. The agency fee is typically baked into the bill rate, not itemized separately, which is worth clarifying upfront.

> **Reality Check:** The most common budgeting mistake is comparing the locum bill rate to a staff physician's base salary. You're paying for credentialing, malpractice, travel logistics, housing, and risk absorption — all of which you'd be managing yourself with a direct hire. The real comparison is bill rate vs. the cost of leaving a shift unfilled.
