## How to Choose a Locum Tenens Agency in San Antonio

- **Verify NALTO membership before the first call.** NALTO members are bound to a code of ethics that includes transparent fee disclosure and complaint resolution — non-members aren't. It takes 30 seconds to confirm on nalto.org and immediately filters out the bottom third of the market.
- **Ask specifically about Texas licensing timelines.** Texas Medical Board processing is not fast. Any agency that can't give you a realistic 60–90 day window for an unlicensed physician — or doesn't have a dedicated licensing coordinator on staff — will blow your go-live date.
- **Demand Joint Commission or NCQA credentialing documentation upfront.** Primary source verification should be a delivered report, not a verbal assurance. If they hesitate to provide documentation, that's your answer.
- **Get the guarantee policy in writing before you sign.** A 30-day placement guarantee is table stakes. Anything shorter, or anything with carve-outs for "specialty availability," is a red flag in a market as competitive as San Antonio.
- **Match the agency's specialty depth to your actual gap.** A generalist firm that dabbles in hospitalist coverage is not the same as one with a 500-provider hospitalist bench. Ask how many placements they've made in your specific specialty in Texas in the last 12 months.

> **Pro Tip:** San Antonio has a significant veteran and active-duty population — roughly 1 in 4 residents has a military connection. If you're covering a facility that sees this patient mix, ask your agency whether their physician bench includes providers with VA or DoD experience. It's a legitimate clinical differentiator that most agencies won't volunteer.

## What to Expect

Locum tenens contracts in San Antonio typically run $20,000–$150,000 depending on specialty, duration, and whether the engagement is nights/weekends or standard daytime coverage — with procedural specialists (interventional cardiology, orthopedic surgery) hitting the high end and primary care or urgent care running closer to $20,000–$45,000 for short-term fills. Most credentialed agencies can get a placed-and-cleared physician on-site within 2–6 weeks for licensed Texas providers; unlicensed physicians add 60–90 days minimum. The agency fee is baked into the hourly rate they bill you — you're not paying it separately.

> **Reality Check:** The most common budget mistake isn't the agency fee — it's forgetting malpractice tail coverage. Most agencies provide occurrence-based coverage during the engagement, but the moment a locum's assignment ends, so does the policy. If an incident surfaces six months later, you need to know who's carrying the tail. Get that in writing before anyone steps foot in your facility.
