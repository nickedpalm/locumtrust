Finding a qualified locum tenens agency in [Manchester](/locations/new-hampshire/manchester/) shouldn't feel like a credential scavenger hunt — but for most CMOs and clinic administrators at Elliot Health System or Catholic Medical Center, that's exactly what it becomes. One call to the wrong agency and you're six weeks into a placement that falls apart at the credentialing stage. This directory cuts through the noise so you can move fast without getting burned.

## How to Choose a Locum Tenens Agency in Manchester

- **Verify NALTO membership first, full stop.** NALTO members are bound by a code of ethics that covers fee transparency, candidate vetting, and non-compete restrictions. Non-members aren't. In a market where New Hampshire's physician shortage hits primary care and psychiatry hardest, the agency roster matters.
- **Ask specifically about New Hampshire licensure timelines.** NH is not a Compact state for physicians (though it joined the Nurse Licensure Compact). That means a locum MD coming from out of state needs a full NH Board of Medicine license — which runs 8–12 weeks on a good day. Any agency quoting you a two-week start date for an unlicensed physician is setting you up for a missed coverage window.
- **Demand primary source verification documentation upfront.** NCQA-credentialed and Joint Commission Certified agencies maintain audit-ready credential files. Ask to see the credentialing checklist before you sign — if they hedge, keep walking.
- **Pin down the malpractice coverage terms.** Occurrence-based vs. claims-made is the difference between "covered forever" and "covered only if the claim is filed during the contract." For surgical and procedural specialties, occurrence-based is the only defensible choice.
- **Get the guarantee policy in writing.** Top agencies offer a prorated refund or free replacement if a placement falls through within the first 30 days. If there's no guarantee, that tells you everything about their confidence in their own vetting.

> **Pro Tip:** Manchester's hospital market is small enough that agencies with a dedicated New England or Northeast desk will outperform national generalists every time. Ask how many placements they've made in NH in the past 18 months. A real answer is a number. "We cover the whole country" is not.

## What to Expect

Locum tenens contracts in Manchester typically run $20,000–$150,000 depending on specialty, duration, and urgency — emergency medicine and psychiatry command the premium end, while general internal medicine coverage sits closer to the floor. Most agencies work on a markup over the locum's daily rate, so the bill rate you see already includes their fee, malpractice, and travel. Expect a 4–8 week lead time for a credentialed, licensed placement in NH; faster is possible if the provider already holds an active NH license.

> **Reality Check:** The biggest pricing mistake administrators make is comparing bill rates across agencies without confirming what's included. Agency A quoting $180/hour might be covering travel, housing, and malpractice. Agency B at $165/hour might be handing you three separate invoices. Get an all-in cost-per-day figure before you benchmark anything.

Manchester is New Hampshire's largest city and the commercial hub of a state with one of the most acute rural physician shortages in New England — over 40% of NH towns are designated Health Professional Shortage Areas, and that pressure concentrates referral volume into Manchester's two major hospital systems. If you're staffing here, you're not just covering shifts; you're backstopping a regional safety net, which means credentialing gaps or no-shows have downstream consequences that ripple into Concord and beyond. Agencies that understand that pressure — and staff accordingly — are worth every dollar of their margin.
