Finding a qualified locum tenens agency in [Chicago](/locations/illinois/chicago/) shouldn't feel like a credential scavenger hunt — but it usually does. The city runs on three major academic medical centers, a sprawling network of safety-net hospitals, and enough specialty referral demand that a single unexpected physician departure can back up patient flow for weeks. This directory cuts through the noise and shows you which agencies have actually placed providers in the Illinois market.

## How to Choose a Locum Tenens Agency in Chicago

- **Check NALTO membership first, not last.** NALTO members are bound to a code of ethics covering transparency in billing, provider qualification, and contract terms. In a market with dozens of generalist staffing firms claiming "locum tenens expertise," it's the fastest filter that costs you nothing to apply.
- **Ask specifically about Illinois licensing turnaround.** Illinois has a notoriously slow medical licensing process — 60 to 90 days is common for a standard application. Agencies that operate regularly in Illinois should have workarounds: existing active licensees on their bench, or relationships that speed up expedited processing. If they can't give you a concrete answer, they don't place here often enough.
- **Verify credentialing accreditation before you sign anything.** NCQA Credentialing Accreditation and Joint Commission Certified Staffing Organization status mean an agency's primary source verification process has been audited by a third party. At any of the Joint Commission–accredited hospitals in the Chicago metro — Northwestern, Rush, University of Chicago — your medical staff office will thank you.
- **Match specialty depth to your actual need.** A generalist agency might fill a hospitalist gap fine. If you need an interventional cardiologist or a pediatric subspecialist, you want an agency whose bench in that specialty is deep enough that they're not just running a cold search on your timeline.
- **Get the malpractice structure in writing upfront.** Most reputable agencies provide occurrence-based or tail-covered claims-made coverage. Confirm the limits match Illinois hospital requirements before a locum is ever credentialed onto your medical staff.

> **Pro Tip:** Ask every agency for a reference from a Chicago-area facility in your same care setting — academic, community, or federally qualified health center. A strong track record placing in suburban Cook County is not the same as a track record at a busy Level I trauma center on the South Side.

## What to Expect

Locum tenens contracts in the Chicago market typically run $20,000 to $150,000 depending on specialty, duration, and coverage intensity — primary care coverage for a rural Illinois community health center looks nothing like a three-month neurosurgery gap at a tertiary center. Agency fees are typically baked into the all-in rate rather than billed separately, though the structure varies. Most established agencies can have a qualified candidate in front of you within five to fifteen business days for common specialties; rarer subspecialties will take longer regardless of how good the agency is.

> **Reality Check:** The lowest quoted rate is almost never the lowest actual cost. Agencies that underquote and then bill for travel, housing, and licensing reimbursements separately will cost you more in reconciliation headaches than the spread was worth. Get a total cost-of-engagement number in writing before you compare bids.

## Local Market Overview

Chicago's healthcare labor market is tight by any measure — the city is simultaneously a major academic training hub and a net exporter of trained physicians to mid-size markets across the Midwest, which means facilities competing for permanent staff are often the same ones most reliant on locums to bridge gaps. Illinois also has one of the more complex regulatory environments for temporary provider licensure in the country, which rewards agencies with genuine in-state infrastructure over firms parachuting in from markets where licensing moves faster.
