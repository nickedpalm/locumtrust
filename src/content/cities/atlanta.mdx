Finding a qualified locum tenens agency in [Atlanta](/locations/georgia/atlanta/) shouldn't require three weeks of cold calls and a law degree — but between Georgia's complex licensing reciprocity rules and Grady Health System's perpetual staffing crunch, most administrators end up choosing whoever called them first. This directory exists so you can compare agencies on the credentials that actually matter before someone's paging you at 6am about a coverage gap.

## How to Choose a Locum Tenens Agency in Atlanta

- **Verify NALTO membership and credentialing accreditation first.** NALTO members are bound to a code of ethics that covers fee transparency and provider vetting. NCQA credentialing accreditation means their primary source verification isn't just a checkbox — it's audited. In Atlanta's competitive market, where Emory, Piedmont, and WellStar are often competing for the same specialists, agencies cutting corners on credentialing will create liability exposure you don't want.
- **Ask specifically about Georgia Medical Composite Board turnaround.** Georgia isn't the slowest licensing state in the country, but it's not the fastest either. A good agency already knows the current processing window and has a locum who's Georgia-licensed in your specialty queued up — not one they need to start licensing from scratch.
- **Nail down the malpractice structure upfront.** Occurrence vs. claims-made coverage isn't a detail to sort out after the provider starts. Reputable agencies carry occurrence-based malpractice for their locums. If they can't answer the question clearly, move on.
- **Require specialty depth, not just specialty breadth.** An agency that claims to place "all 40 specialties" is usually a generalist shop with a thin bench in most of them. For high-acuity placements — hospitalist, emergency medicine, surgical subspecialties — ask how many Georgia-licensed providers they have active in your specialty right now.
- **Get the guarantee policy in writing.** The best agencies offer a pro-rated refund or replacement if a placed provider doesn't work out within the first few shifts. If they won't commit to it in the contract, their confidence in their own vetting process is telling you something.

> **Pro Tip:** Atlanta's Hartsfield-[Jackson](/locations/mississippi/jackson/) airport is a genuine asset here — it's one of the few markets where an agency can realistically fly in a locum from anywhere in the country for even a short-duration engagement without the travel logistics becoming the whole project.

## What to Expect

Locum tenens contracts in Atlanta typically run $20,000–$150,000 depending on specialty, duration, and whether the agency is covering housing and travel. Primary care and family medicine placements sit toward the lower end; surgical subspecialties and emergency medicine push the ceiling. Most agencies can turn around a qualified candidate within 48–72 hours for common specialties, though credentialing and privileging at your facility will add 2–4 weeks to the clock regardless of how fast the agency moves.

> **Reality Check:** The quoted daily rate is not your total cost. Agency fee, malpractice coverage, housing stipend, travel, and any licensing fees the agency fronts are all real line items. Administrators who budget on the day rate alone and then get the invoice tend to have difficult conversations with finance. Ask for a fully-loaded cost estimate before you approve the placement.

## Local Market Overview

Atlanta is one of the densest healthcare markets in the Southeast — home to the CDC, a major academic medical center in Emory, and a sprawling network of regional hospitals and FQHCs serving a metro population of over six million. That concentration means demand for locum coverage is high year-round, particularly in Fulton and DeKalb counties where patient volumes consistently outpace permanent staffing pipelines. Agencies with existing relationships in Georgia, rather than ones who'll be calling Atlanta providers cold on your behalf, will get you to coverage faster.
