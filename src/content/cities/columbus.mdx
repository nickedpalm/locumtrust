## How to Choose a Locum Tenens Agency in Columbus

- **Verify NALTO membership first.** NALTO members are bound by a code of ethics on fee transparency, candidate vetting, and contract terms. In a market like Columbus — where OhioHealth, Nationwide Children's, and The Ohio State University Wexner Medical Center all compete for the same pool of credentialed hospitalists — an agency without NALTO standing is a red flag before you've even seen a CV.
- **Ask specifically about Ohio licensing timelines.** Ohio's Medical Board processes endorsement applications in 4–8 weeks under normal load, but expedited pathways exist. An agency that can't tell you their average Ohio credentialing turnaround isn't doing primary source verification at scale — they're farming it out and hoping.
- **Match specialty depth to your gap.** Columbus has a genuine shortage in rural-adjacent specialties — psychiatry, general surgery, and critical care fill slowest. Ask agencies for their active candidate counts in your specific specialty, not their total database size. Five credentialed, available hospitalists beats 50,000 names in a spreadsheet.
- **Confirm malpractice structure in writing.** Ohio is a claims-made state. Make sure the agency's coverage includes tail, and get the policy limits in the contract — not a verbal assurance from a recruiter who won't be answering your calls at 11pm when something goes sideways.
- **Check Joint Commission certification or NCQA credentialing accreditation.** These aren't vanity badges — they mean an independent auditor has verified the agency's credentialing process meets national standards. For a Magnet-designated hospital or a health system under CMS scrutiny, this isn't optional.

> **Pro Tip:** Columbus's academic medical center concentration means agencies with GME relationships often have faster pipelines for subspecialists. Ask if they've placed at Wexner or Nationwide Children's before — it's a proxy for whether they know the local credentialing office's actual preferences.

## What to Expect

Locum tenens contracts in Columbus typically run $20,000–$150,000 depending on specialty, duration, and urgency — a weekend hospitalist fill runs toward the low end; a six-month cardiothoracic surgery engagement is a different conversation entirely. Agency fees (typically 25–40% on top of physician compensation) are usually embedded in the all-in rate, not broken out, which is exactly where confusion starts.

> **Reality Check:** The most common billing mistake isn't overpaying for the physician — it's underestimating the ancillary costs: credentialing fees, travel, housing, and malpractice tail. Get a fully loaded cost estimate in writing before signing. Agencies that won't provide one aren't giving you a deal; they're giving you a surprise invoice.

## Local Market Overview

Columbus is Ohio's largest city and one of the fastest-growing metros in the Midwest, with a healthcare labor market shaped by a major academic medical center, two dominant health systems, and significant Federally Qualified Health Center presence serving underinsured populations on the south and east sides. That combination — prestige institutions competing with safety-net demand — means locum coverage gaps appear fast and fill slow, making agency credentialing speed and specialty bench depth the variables that actually determine whether your department stays open.
