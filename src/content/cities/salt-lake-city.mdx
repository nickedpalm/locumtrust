## How to Choose a Locum Tenens Agency in Salt Lake City

- **Verify NALTO membership first, then ask about Utah-specific licensing.** Utah is a Compact state for NPs and PAs, but physician licensing through the Utah Division of Occupational and Professional Licensing still takes 4–8 weeks. Any agency worth your time knows this timeline cold and has a credentialing coordinator who's navigated it before — not one who's going to figure it out alongside you.
- **Ask directly about Intermountain Health and U of U familiarity.** These two systems dominate Salt Lake's inpatient volume. An agency with active relationships in those credentialing offices can cut days off your onboarding timeline. An agency that's never placed anyone there is starting from scratch at your expense.
- **Check their malpractice coverage structure.** Occurrence-based coverage is the standard for locum placements; claims-made with a tail rider is a red flag. Get the coverage limits in writing before you discuss a candidate — $1M/$3M minimum for most specialties, higher for surgical.
- **Specialty depth matters more than agency size.** A large national firm with a thin hospitalist bench will underserve you. Ask for the number of active locum physicians in your specialty currently available in Mountain Time Zone — not their total national database.
- **Get the guarantee policy in writing.** Top agencies guarantee 90-day replacement at no additional cost if a placement doesn't work out. If they won't commit to that in the contract, that tells you something about how much they trust their own vetting process.

> **Pro Tip:** Salt Lake City's elevation (4,226 ft) is a legitimate consideration for locum physicians relocating from sea level. Agencies placing candidates for physically demanding specialties — emergency medicine, hospitalist — should proactively screen for this. The good ones already do.

## What to Expect

Locum contracts in Salt Lake City typically run $20,000–$150,000 depending on specialty, duration, and whether you're covering a single weekend or a 90-day hospitalist gap while a permanent hire clears credentialing. Agencies bundle their fee into the bill rate, so you're not writing a separate placement check — but that also means the per-hour or per-shift rate you're quoted is doing double duty.

> **Reality Check:** Administrators frequently compare agency bill rates without accounting for what's included. One agency quoting $185/hour might be covering malpractice, housing, and travel. Another at $165/hour might not. Build a total-cost-per-shift model before you declare a winner on price.

Agency timelines for qualified candidates typically run 48–72 hours for an initial candidate slate; full credentialing and privileges, 3–6 weeks depending on the facility's medical staff office and the physician's prior Utah licensure history.
